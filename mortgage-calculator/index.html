How to use this mortgage calculator

Enter your home price and adjust the down payment using either a dollar amount or a percentage. Set your loan term (typically 15 or 30 years) and the annual interest rate. To get a realistic total monthly payment, check "Include Taxes & Costs" and add your local property tax rate, homeowners insurance, and any HOA fees.

What is PMI?

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is required by most lenders when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price — your loan-to-value ratio exceeds 80%. PMI typically costs 0.5–1.5% of the loan amount annually and can be cancelled once you reach 20% equity.

Understanding the amortization schedule

Early in your loan, most of each payment goes toward interest. Over time, the split shifts — more goes toward principal and less toward interest. This is called amortization. The schedule above shows this breakdown month by month or year by year for your entire loan term.
